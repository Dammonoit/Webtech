A Brief History of Time

 -by
Stephen Hawking

A Brief History of Time is a popular-science book that helps a layman understand the basic questions of our existence and physics. It is a scientific attempt at explaining the origin of the universe. The author has collated theories created by varying cultures over the years, taking you on a historic and scientific journey. The book introduces the theory of relativity, quantum physics, particle physics, gravity and the fabric of space-time in the first half. Complicated concepts like these are explained and Hawking shows us how Einstein's work had a lasting effect on how you see the universe. Einstein introduced the fourth dimension, time, into the three dimensional universe. A Brief History of Time also covers quantum mechanics and the book goes on to delve into the author's research. Written in a style that is simple and easy to understand, this book is sure to broaden your horizon.

About the author:

Stephen Hawking is a British theoretical physicist, mathematician, cosmologist and Director of Research at the Centre for Theoretical Cosmology at the University of Cambridge. He was born in Oxford, England in 1942. He held the position of Lucasian Professor of Mathematics at Cambridge for many years and still has an office at the Department for Applied Maths and Theoretical Physics. He has been working towards creating a theory that unifies general relativity with quantum theory. He has won many awards and is the recipient of 12 honorary degrees. He was diagnosed with Acquiring Locked-in Syndrome in 1963 which has forced him to move around using a wheelchair and he uses a computerized voice system in order to communicate. His other works include The Grand Design, The Universe in A Nutshell and Black Holes And Baby Universes And Other Essays.
